Spicy Gouda Chicken Pasta Salad

Ingredients

  • 1/2 C May
  • 1/4 C White Vinegar
  • 21/4 C Whole Milk
  • 1 Can Chipotle Peppers in Adobo Sauce, Adobo sauce separated from Peppers
  • 1-2 Boxes Penne Noodles, (Dependent on how sauced you want your noodles)
  • 2 Chicken breast, raw
  • 1 carton Grape Tomatoes, halved
  • 1 Bag Spinach, Fresh and cut up
  • 3 TBS and 1 TBS Basil, fresh
  • 1 C Gouda cheese, shredded or cubed
  • Olive oil for pan
  • Cayenne
  • Chili Powder
  • Tony Chacher's Creole Seasoning
  • Garlic Salt
  • Pepper
  • 1 Bell Pepper (optional), diced
  • 1 Cucumber (optional), diced

Directions

  1. Dice raw chicken and put in olive oiled pan with chipotle peppers.
  2. Season chicken with creole and garlic salt. Cook on medium until tender. Cool chicken.
  3. Boil water and cook Penne noodles with a dash of creole seasoning and salt in the water. It should taste like spicy ocean water. Drain when noodles are tender. Cool noodles.
  4. Remove chipotle peppers from chicken. These will not be in the final dish.
  5. In a separate bowl, combine milk, adobo sauce, white vinegar, 1 TBS Basil and mayo by whisking.
  6. Combine chicken (with peppers taken out), noodles, sauce from step 5, grape tomatoes, spinach, 3 TBS Basil, cheese, bell pepper (optional), cucumber (optional) and seasonings (to your personal taste).
  7. Best served cool.

Extra Notes for Success

  • An easy way to separate the chipotle peppers and the adobo sauce is to make the dressing from step five and pour entire can of peppers into the sauce, then strain the sauce. Keep what was separated in the strainer and the bowl. What's in the bowl is now the dressing and what's in the strainer is ready to be cooked with the chicken.
  • The dressing for the salad will be liquidy. If you want a thicker sauce, you can add 1/4 C of grated parmesan cheese (the kind that comes in the shake can).
  • Bell Peppers and Cucumber is optional. I normally do not include this.
  • For more spice, double the adobo sauce or add more cayenne.
