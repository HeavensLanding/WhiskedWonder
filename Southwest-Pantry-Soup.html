Southwest Pantry Soup

Ingredients

  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 can red beans
  • 1 can navy beans
  • 1 can corn (NOT creamed corn)
  • 1 can Rotel tomatoes and chiles
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (any type)
  • 12 oz chicken broth
  • 2 chicken breasts, cooked and shredded
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 3 bell peppers, diced (red, yellow, green)
  • 2 cloves garlic or 2 TBS minced and jarred garlic
  • 16oz tub Cottage cheese
  • Jalapeño or other peppers (optional)
  • Cilantro, chopped (topping)
  • Avocado, diced (topping)
  • Tortilla strips (topping)
  • Shredded cheese (topping)

Directions

  1. Drain all beans but don't rinse
  2. While beans are draining, sauté onion with butter or some form of oil.
  3. Once onion is almost done, add garlic, bell peppers and if using peppers, add as well.
  4. All veggies in pan must be tender. Add chicken and stir. Cook together for 5 mins.
  5. While step four is occurring, retrieve beans.
  6. Add the rest of the canned ingredients and broth in.
  7. Let cook 10 mins on low/medium, stir occasionally.
  8. Season soup while step 7 is occurring.
  9. Add cottage cheese, more or less, depending on how creamy you want. Milk/buttermilk/heavy cream is also an option on this step. This also lessens the heat if you made it too spicy. Cook down the dairy before step 10.
  10. Add toppings and serve

Extra Notes for Success

  • If freezing, complete all steps above except garnishment. Freeze in ziplock bag. When ready for eating, defrost by running hot water on the sides of the bag until loosened. Slide contents into pot on the stove and heat on medium until warmed. Top and serve.
  • Other veggies that can be added include: carrots, cauliflower, squash, more tomatoes, etc. This makes it more of a fall soup. Do NOT use celery, cucumber and other veggies of that nature.
